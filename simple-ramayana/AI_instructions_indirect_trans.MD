### 1. Grammatical Rules
*   **Past Participles for Past Tense:** Always use past participles (*Kṛdanta / Kta pratyaya*) for past tense actions instead of finite verbs (e.g., using *bhūtaḥ / bhūtā / bhūtam* instead of *āsīt*). 
*   **Passive and Impersonal Voice:** Convert past tense sentences to the passive (*karmaṇi prayoga*) or impersonal passive (*bhāve prayoga*) voice to naturally make use of these past participles.
*   **Pronoun Clarification (Parentheses):** Whenever a pronoun (such as *saḥ*, *tena*, *tasyai*) or an implicit referent represents a main character, explicitly clarify it by attaching the name in parentheses directly after the pronoun (e.g., `सः (दशरथः)`). Ensure there is only one such parenthetical mention per sentence to avoid redundancy.
*   **No "मे" (me):** Avoid the enclitic *me* (*me*). Exclusively use the explicit forms **मम** (*mama* - for Genitive/my) or **मह्यम्** (*mahyam* - for Dative/to me) to maintain simple and clear Sanskrit.
*   **No "च" (ca) for Single-Entity Adjectives:** Do not use "च" to join multiple adjectives or vocative terms that describe the same individual. Instead, place them side-by-side in apposition.
*   **Introductory "अथ" (atha) for Questions:** Start introductory questions that contain "Why then" or "How indeed" with **अथ** (*atha*) instead of *tarhi*.

### 2. Bracketing and Formatting Rules
*   **Strict Bracketing:** Maintain square brackets `[]` around the Sanskrit adjectives and participles.
*   **No Partial-Word Bracketing:** If a Sanskrit translation uses a compound word for a bracketed English term, the square brackets must wrap around the entire compound word (e.g., `[पूर्णकामा]` rather than `[पूर्ण]कामा`).
*   **Speaker Tag Formatting:** Format direct spoken dialogue strictly as:  
    `Speaker - "Quote"`  
    *(Use a space, a hyphen, and a space. Do not use bold markdown or colons).*
*   **Alternating Block Layout:** Present the entire translation block-by-block in the following order:
    *   Verse Number (e.g., `2.13.1`)
    *   Sanskrit Sentence 1
    *   English Sentence 1
    *   Sanskrit Sentence 2
    *   English Sentence 2
