### 1. Structure & Formatting
*   **Verse Number**: Place the verse number (e.g., 1.48.1) at the top.
*   **Sentence Structure**: Convert each line of the original verse into one independent, standalone Sanskrit sentence.
*   **Formatting**: Use no bullets, numbering, or headers. 
*   **Punctuation**: End every Sanskrit sentence with a danda (।).
*   **Translation**: Provide the English translation directly under each Sanskrit sentence.

### 2. Speaker Identification
*   **Sanskrit**: Name in the Nominative case followed by a hyphen before every sentence (e.g., `रामः -`). 
*   **Narrator**: If the narrator is a character (like Vishvamitra), explicitly use their name before the sentence for clarity, except when the original Author, Valmiki, is speaking
*   **English**: Name followed by a hyphen before the translation (e.g., `Rama -`).

### 3. Grammar Rules (only when original verse is in Past Tense)
*   **General Past Tense**: Use **Passive Voice** with the *kta* pratyaya (e.g., *kṛtam*, *dṛṣṭam*).
*   **'To be' / 'To become'**: Use the **Participle form** (*bhūtaḥ / bhūtā / bhūtam*). Do not use *abhūt* or *āsīt*
*   **in case of gerunds**: if an action consiststs of a gerund end with periphrastic active construction (*tavatu + abhūt*, e.g., *kṛtavān abhūt*)
*   **Verbs of Motion**: Use **Active Voice** with the *kta* pratyaya (e.g., *gataḥ*, *prāptaḥ*, *āgataḥ*). 
*   **Verbs of Speaking**: Use **Active Voice** with the **Aorist** form (*avocat*).
*   **Exclusions**: Do not use *liṭ* (perfect) or *laṅ* (imperfect) verb forms.

### 4. Character Names & Brackets
*   **Aliases/Adjectives**: Replace the alias or descriptive title with a simplified synonym and add the **Proper Name** immediately after in square brackets (e.g., `[रघुवंशज] राम`).
*   **Proper Names**: Do not put brackets around proper names that appear in the verse.
*   **Pronouns**: Do not add the character's name after personal pronouns (like *tena*, *mayā*, *tava*).

### 5. Vocabulary & Clarity
*   **Direct Substitution**: Remove complex or archaic words and replace them with simplified synonyms.
*   **Marking Adjectives**: Use **[square brackets]** to mark adjectives and aliases.
*   **Additions**: Use **(parentheses)** for words added for clarity or context that are not explicitly in the original text (e.g., adding the subject if it was implied).
*   **Gender**: Use **Neuter Gender** for pronouns/adjectives referring to weapons (*astrāṇi*).

### 6. Orthography (Sandhi Rules)
*   **The M/Anusvara Rule**: 
    *   Use a final **'m' (म्)** when the next word starts with a **vowel**.
    *   Use **Anusvara (ं)** when the next word starts with a **consonant**.
    *   Example: `वाक्यम् अवोचत्` vs `वाक्यं पृष्टवान्`.

### 7. Translation Style
*   Provide a literal but fluid English translation.
*   Reflect the simplified structure of the Sanskrit sentence exactly.
*   Include the speaker's name at the start of the translation if it was included in the Sanskrit.


#### 8. Voice & Tense

* **Passive Voice (*Karmani*):** Use the **-ta** suffix (*kta* pratyaya) for past actions (e.g., *kṛtam*, *dṛṣṭam*, *uktam*) rather than active forms like *uvāca* or *dadarśa*.
* **Rule of Motion:** Verbs of motion (Rule 3 in our previous logic) should remain in the **Active Voice** (*Kartari*) using the Nominative case for the subject.

#### 9. Formatting & Syntax

* **Square Brackets `[]`:** Used **strictly for adjectives and descriptors** only.
* **Parentheses `()`:** Used for **implicit words** (words added for grammatical clarity that aren't in the original verse).
* **Bare Text:** Nouns, verbs, and core subjects stay without brackets.
* **Prose Order (Anvaya):** Reorganize the verse into a standard **Subject-Object-Verb** sequence.

#### 10. Orthography & Grammar

* **Anusvara Rule:** Never place an **anusvara (ं)** before a word starting with a vowel; use the final **'m' (म्)** instead.
* **Enclitic Replacement:** Replace all shortened enclitic pronouns (*te, me, naḥ, vaḥ*) with their full forms (*tubhyam/tava, mama, asmākam, yuṣmākam*) to avoid case ambiguity.
* **Case Consistency:** Every adjective or alias must match the exact case of the noun it modifies (e.g., if the subject is *viśvāmitreṇa*, the adjective must be *mahātejasā*).

#### 11. Simplification

* **Compound Dissolution:** Break long compounds (*samāsa*) into individual words separated by hyphens or distinct words (e.g., *nānā-vṛkṣa-yuktam*).
* **Sandhi Neutralization:** Remove internal sandhi between separate words to make the breakdown easier to read (e.g., write *tataḥ āśramam* instead of *tato'śramam*).
* **Explicit Clarity:** Use explicit names or clarified pronouns instead of ambiguous ones to maintain context.
