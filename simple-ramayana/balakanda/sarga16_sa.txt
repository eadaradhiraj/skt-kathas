1.16.1
Sanskrit Prose
ततः [सुरसत्तमैः] नियुक्तः नारायणः देवः, जानन् अपि, एवं सुरान् [श्लक्ष्णं] वचनम् उक्तवान् अभूत्।
Then Lord Narayana, having been appointed by the best of gods, although knowing everything, spoke this gentle word to the gods.

1.16.2
विष्णुः - "[दुरात्मनः] तस्य रावणस्य वधे कः उपायः अस्ति? येन उपायेन अहम् [ऋषिकण्टकम्] तं निहन्याम्।"
Vishnu - "What is the strategy for the destruction of that evil-souled Ravana, by which I may slay that thorn to the sages?"

1.16.3
एवम् उक्ताः सर्वे सुराः [अव्ययम्] विष्णुं प्रति उक्तवन्तः अभूवन् - "त्वं मानव-रूपं स्वीकृत्य, युद्धे रावणं जहि।"
Having been spoken to thus, all the gods replied to the imperishable Vishnu: "Having accepted a human form, slay Ravana in battle."

The Gods - "O destroyer of foes, that Ravana performed intense penance for a long time, by which penance Brahma, the creator of the worlds and the first-born of the worlds, was pleased."

1.16.5

The Gods - "The satisfied Lord Brahma granted a boon to that rakshasa that he would have no fear from various kinds of beings, except from a human."

1.16.6
देवाः - "पुरा वरदाне तेन मानवाः अवज्ञाताः। एवं तस्मात् पितामहात् वरं प्राप्य, सः रावणः दर्पितः अभूत्।"
The Gods - "Formerly, at the time of the boon-giving, humans were disregarded by him. Thus, having obtained that boon from the Grandsire, Ravana became arrogant."

1.16.7
देवाः - "सः त्रीन् लोकान् उत्सादयति, स्त्रियः च अपकर्षति। तस्मात्, हे [परन्तप], तस्य वधः मानुषेभ्यः एव निश्चितः अस्ति।"
The Gods - "He torments the three worlds and carries off women. Therefore, O tormentor of foes, his death is certain only from humans."

1.16.8

Having heard these words of the gods, the self-possessed Vishnu then chose King Dasharatha as his father.

1.16.9

At that time, that sonless king Dasharatha, who was brilliant, a destroyer of enemies, and desiring a son, was performing the Putriya sacrifice.

That being was clad in black and crimson garments, was red-faced, and had a voice like a drum. His whiskers were soft like a lion's mane, and his hair was excellent.

1.16.13
तत् भूतम् [शुभैः लक्षणैः सम्पन्नः], [दिव्यैः आभरणैः भूषितः] च अभूत्। सः शैलशृङ्गम् इव उत्तुङ्गः अभूत्, तस्य विक्रमः च दृप्तस्य शार्दूलस्य इव अभूत्।
That being was endowed with auspicious signs and adorned with divine ornaments. He was tall like a mountain peak, and his stride was like that of an arrogant tiger.

The form of that being was equal to the sun, and that being was like the crest of a blazing fire. The vessel held by him was made of molten Jambunada gold, and its lid was made of silver.

By that being, with a pair of hands, a vessel was held like a beloved wife, which was as if made by magic, filled with divine payasam, and large.

1.16.16

That being, looking at King Dasharatha, spoke this word: "O King, know me to be a man from Prajapati, come here."

Then the man from Prajapati spoke this word again: "O King, by you who have been worshiping the gods, this has been obtained today."

1.16.19

The man from Prajapati said: "O tiger among kings, accept this payasam, which is prepared by the gods, confers progeny, bestows affluence, and improves health."
