1.18.1
[महात्मनः] राज्ञः अश्वमेध-यागे निर्वृत्ते सति, सुराः भागान् प्रतिगृह्य, यथागतं प्रतिगताः।
When the Ashvamedha sacrifice of the great-souled king was completed, the gods, having received their shares, returned from whence they came.

1.18.2
[समाप्त-दीक्षा-नियमः] [पत्नीगण-समन्वितः] [सभृत्य-बल-वाहनः] राजा दशरथः पुरीं प्रविष्टवान् अभूत्।
Having completed the rules of initiation, King Dasharatha, accompanied by his group of wives and with his servants, army, and vehicles, entered the city.

1.18.3
तेन राज्ञा यथार्हं पूजिताः [पृथिवीश्वराः] [मुदिताः] भूत्वा, [मुनिपुङ्गवं] वसिष्ठं प्रणम्य, स्वदेशान् प्रति गताः।
Those lords of the earth, having been honored in a fitting manner by the king and being pleased, returned to their own countries after bowing to the best of sages, Vasistha.

1.18.4
ततः पुरात् स्वपुराणि गच्छतां तेषां [श्रीमतां] राज्ञाम्, [शुभ्राणि] [प्रहृष्टानि] बलानि शोभितानि अभूवन्।
As the armies of those glorious kings were departing from the city to their own cities, their bright and cheerful forces shone.

1.18.5
पृथिवीश्वराणां गमनात् परं, तदा [श्रीमान्] राजा दशरथः [द्विजोत्तमान्] पुरस्कृत्य, पुरीं प्रविष्टः।
After the departure of the kings, the glorious King Dasharatha then entered the city, placing the best of Brahmins at the forefront.

1.18.6
अथ [सुपूजितः] ऋष्यशृङ्गः, [धीमता] [सानुयात्रेण] राज्ञा रोमपादेन अन्वीयमानः सन्, शान्तया सार्धं गतः।
Then the well-honored Rishyashringa, being accompanied by the wise King Romapada and his followers, departed with Shanta.

1.18.7
राजा दशरथः एवं तान् सर्वान् विसृज्य, [सम्पूर्णमानसः] भूत्वा, पुत्रोत्पत्तिं विचिन्तयन्, तत्र सुखितः उषितवान् अभूत्।
Having thus sent them all away, King Dasharatha, with a fulfilled mind, lived there happily, contemplating the birth of his sons.

1.18.8
ततः यज्ञे समाप्ते सति षट् ऋतवः समतीताः अभूवन्। ततः द्वादशः मासः, चैत्रः मासः, नवमी तिथिः च अभूत्।
Then, after the sacrifice was completed, six seasons passed. It was then the twelfth month, the month of Chaitra, and the ninth lunar day.

1.18.9
अदिति-दैवत्यं नक्षत्रम् अभूत्। पञ्च ग्रहाः स्वोच्च-स्थिताः अभूवन्। कर्कटः लग्नः अभूत्, वाक्पतिः च इन्दुना सह प्रोद्यमानः अभूत्।
The star was Punarvasu, whose deity is Aditi. Five planets were in their exalted positions. The ascendant was Cancer, and Jupiter was rising with the Moon.

1.18.10
तस्मिन् काले कौसल्या [जगन्नाथं], [सर्वलोकनमस्कृतं], [सर्वलक्षणसंयुतं], [विष्णोः अर्धं], [महाभागं], [ऐक्ष्वाकुवर्धनं] रामं नाम पुत्रं जनितवती अभूत्।
At that time, Kausalya gave birth to a son named Rama, who was the lord of the universe, honored by all the worlds, endowed with all auspicious signs, a portion of Vishnu himself, greatly fortunate, and the perpetuator of the Ikshvaku race.
